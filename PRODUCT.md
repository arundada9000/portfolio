# PRODUCT.md — Arun Neupane Portfolio

> Strategic context for the impeccable design skill. Answers who / what / why.
> Paired with [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md) (how it looks).

## Register

**Brand.** This is a personal portfolio — the design *is* the product. Craft, identity,
and point of view carry as much weight as the content. Optimize for a memorable,
opinionated experience, not a neutral information delivery.

## Target users

Two audiences, weighted equally — the design must serve both without splitting in two:

1. **Recruiters & hiring managers** (full-time roles). Skim first, depth on demand. They
   need to see real deployed systems, technical range, and enough signal to say "interview
   this person" inside 30 seconds.
2. **Freelance & consulting clients** (project work). Buyers, not engineers. They need
   outcomes, proof it shipped, and an obvious, low-friction way to start a conversation.

Secondary: peers and the Nepal developer community (open-source credibility, teaching).

## Product purpose

Convert a cold visitor into a warm lead — an interview request or a project inquiry —
by proving, not claiming, that Arun ships production-grade web systems. The portfolio
itself is the strongest work sample: if the site is impeccable, the pitch is already made.

**Primary outcome:** a contact-form submission or direct email.
**Secondary outcome:** a click into a case study or GitHub, deepening credibility.

## What to lead with

A deliberate *combination*, in this priority when space forces a choice:

1. **Production systems** — real deployed impact (Nepal Police case-management system,
   the national-NGO WebAuthn biometric platform, Sajilo Digital, Sajilo Sahayata,
   Roomeo). Proof over polish. This is the recruiter and client hook.
2. **Design & craft** — the terminal "night shift" identity and its signature statusline
   demonstrate front-end capability the moment the page loads. Show, don't tell.
3. **Range & versatility** — 70+ repos, frontend-to-full-stack, algorithm visualizers,
   tools, company sites, teaching curricula. Evidence of dedication and self-driven growth.

These are not three separate sections competing for attention; they are one argument —
*a self-taught developer from Nepal who builds real things at a high bar* — told through
projects (1), embodied in the site's craft (2), and backed by breadth (3).

## Brand personality

- **Self-made and unpretentious.** Learned in public, shipped everything, codes past
  midnight. Confidence earned through output, not credentials.
- **Technical and precise.** A developer's aesthetic — terminal, monospace, command-line
  literacy — used as genuine identity, not costume.
- **Warm underneath the machine.** Nepal-rooted, community-minded, a little playful
  (the live NPT clock, the "still awake at 1 AM" line). Human, not corporate.

Voice: direct, plain verbs, lowercase-terminal cadence for UI chrome, full sentences for
substance. Never salesy, never falsely humble.

## Anti-references (reject on sight)

All four were explicitly rejected by Arun:

1. **Generic template look** — stock hero, three service cards, interchangeable
   Bootstrap/Framer-template shells with no point of view.
2. **Corporate & safe** — muted, minimal, inoffensive agency styling that reads as
   forgettable and risk-averse.
3. **Over-animated / flashy** — scroll-jacking, gratuitous motion, effects that slow the
   page or bury the content. Motion must earn its place.
4. **Trendy AI-portfolio clichés** — the cream-background serif + terracotta accent, and
   the single-neon-on-black look that every AI-generated site now shares.

## Strategic design principles

- **One signature, everything else disciplined.** The vim statusline is the one
  memorable risk. The rest stays quiet so it lands.
- **Prove with specifics.** Every project states a real outcome and links to live + code.
  No vague "passionate about clean code" filler.
- **Fast and accessible is non-negotiable.** Keyboard-navigable, reduced-motion respected,
  contrast verified. Craft includes the parts you don't see.
- **Recruiter-skimmable, client-persuasive, peer-credible — in one read.** Hierarchy lets
  each audience find its path without a mode switch.
